Understanding the Basics: The Wheel and Table Layout

Before you place your first bet, it’s essential to understand the fundamentals of the game. An online casino roulette live table might look complex at first, but it’s quite simple once you break it down.

The game consists of two main components: the wheel and the betting table. The wheel features numbered pockets, from 1 to 36, which are alternately colored red and black. There is also one or more green pockets for the number zero. The dealer spins the wheel in one direction and launches a small ball in the opposite direction. Your goal is to predict which numbered pocket the ball will land in.

The main difference you’ll encounter is between wheel types:

  • European Roulette: This is the most common and player-friendly version. The wheel has 37 pockets: numbers 1-36 and a single green ‘0’. The house edge is a favorable 2.7%.

  • American Roulette: This version is popular in North American casinos. The wheel has 38 pockets: numbers 1-36, a single ‘0’, and a double ‘00’. This extra green pocket significantly increases the house edge to 5.26%. For this reason, knowledgeable players almost always prefer the European version when available.

  • French Roulette: This variant uses a European-style single-zero wheel but includes special rules like “La Partage” or “En Prison.” These rules can give you back half or all of your stake on even-money bets if the ball lands on zero, reducing the house edge even further to just 1.35% on those specific bets.

When you’re ready to play live roulette online, always look for a European or French table to give yourself the best possible odds of winning.

Mastering the Game: A Deep Dive into Roulette Bets

The beauty of roulette live online lies in the variety of bets you can make. You can be conservative with low-risk, even-money wagers, or you can go for glory with a high-payout bet on a single number. These bets are categorized into two main groups: Inside Bets and Outside Bets.

Inside Bets: The High-Risk, High-Reward Wagers

Inside bets are placed on the numbered section of the betting layout. They are considered higher risk because the probability of them hitting is lower, but they offer much larger payouts.

  • Straight Up: A bet on a single number. This is the riskiest bet but offers the highest payout of 35 to 1.

  • Split: A bet on two adjacent numbers, placed on the line between them. Payout is 17 to 1.

  • Street: A bet on a row of three numbers, placed on the outer line of that row. Payout is 11 to 1.

  • Corner (or Square): A bet on a block of four numbers, placed on the corner where the four numbers meet. Payout is 8 to 1.

  • Six Line (or Double Street): A bet on two adjacent rows of three numbers each (six numbers total). Payout is 5 to 1.

Outside Bets: The Steady and Stable Choices

Outside bets are placed on the sections outside the main number grid. They cover large groups of numbers, making them much more likely to win, but they offer smaller payouts. These are ideal for beginners or players who prefer a more conservative strategy.

  • Red or Black: A bet on whether the winning number will be red or black. Payout is 1 to 1.

  • Odd or Even: A bet on whether the winning number will be odd or even. Payout is 1 to 1.

  • High or Low (1-18 or 19-36): A bet on whether the winning number will be in the low range or the high range. Payout is 1 to 1.

  • Dozens: A bet on one of three groups of twelve numbers (1-12, 13-24, or 25-36). Payout is 2 to 1.

  • Columns: A bet on one of the three vertical columns of twelve numbers on the layout. Payout is 2 to 1.
